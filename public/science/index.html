Metabolism & Nutrition

5 studies
Your metabolic rate genuinely changes across your cycle. The cravings are real. The calorie increase is real. PeakHer's nutrition and fasting intelligence is built on this.
Effect of menstrual cycle on resting metabolism: A systematic review and meta-analysis
Hamidovic A, Kaempfe K, Guo X
PLoS ONE, 2020 | DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0236025
Meta-analysis of 26 studies (318 women) finding a significant increase in resting metabolic rate during the luteal phase (~58 kcal/day in ovulatory cycles).
Changes in energy expenditure during the menstrual cycle
Bisdee JT, James WPT, Shaw MA
British Journal of Nutrition, 1989 | DOI: 10.1079/bjn19890108
Metabolic rate during sleep is lowest in the late follicular phase and highest in the late luteal phase, measured under controlled conditions.
Dietary energy intake across the menstrual cycle: a narrative review
Gorczyca AM, Sjaarda LA, Mitchell EM, et al.
Nutrition Reviews, 2023 | PMID: 36367830
Energy intake is generally lower in the follicular phase and higher in the luteal phase, with mean increases of 90-500+ kcal/day. Progesterone counteracts estrogen's appetite-suppressing effect.
Iron deficiency and iron deficiency anemia in women
Percy L, Mansour D, Fraser IS
Scandinavian Journal of Clinical and Laboratory Investigation, 2014 | DOI: 10.3109/00365513.2014.936694
High menstrual blood loss is the most significant contributor to iron deficiency in reproductive-age women.
Structural equation modeling of food craving across the menstrual cycle
Krishnan S, Tryon RR, Horn WF, Welch L, Keim NL
Published 2018 | PMC: 6251416
Progesterone and disinhibition are positively associated with carbohydrate cravings during the luteal phase, providing a mechanistic link between hormones and food cravings.
Dot's translation Your body burns more calories in the second half of your cycle. The chocolate craving is a magnesium request. The bread craving is a glucose request. Your body is talking. I just translate.

Exercise & Performance

5 studies
Your hormonal phase affects injury risk, recovery capacity, and how your body responds to different types of training. PeakHer matches your workout to your biology.
The Effects of Menstrual Cycle Phase on Exercise Performance in Eumenorrheic Women: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
McNulty KL, Elliott-Sale KJ, Dolan E, et al.
Sports Medicine, 2020 | DOI: 10.1007/s40279-020-01319-3
Exercise performance may be trivially reduced during the early follicular (menstrual) phase. Individual variation dwarfs any group-level cycle effect, supporting personalized approaches.
Effects of the Menstrual Cycle on Anterior Cruciate Ligament Injury Risk: A Systematic Review
Hewett TE, Zazulak BT, Myer GD
American Journal of Sports Medicine, 2007 | DOI: 10.1177/0363546506295699
Increased ACL injury risk during the preovulatory phase, when estrogen surges affect ligament laxity and collagen properties.
Myths and Methodologies: Reducing scientific design ambiguity in studies comparing sexes and/or menstrual cycle phases
Sims ST, Heather AK
Experimental Physiology, 2018 | DOI: 10.1113/EP086797
Biphasic estrogen and progesterone responses significantly influence physiological responses and exercise capacity. Research must account for these differences.
Body Composition Over the Menstrual and Oral Contraceptive Cycle in Trained Females
Barba-Moreno L, Cupeiro R, et al. (IronFEMME Study)
International Journal of Sports Physiology and Performance, 2021 | DOI: 10.1123/ijspp.2020-0038
Hormonal fluctuations across the menstrual cycle affect body composition measurements in trained women. Cycle-phase considerations are relevant for accurate assessment.
Menstrual cycle phase does not influence muscle protein synthesis in response to resistance exercise
Murach KA et al.
Journal of Physiology, 2024 | DOI: 10.1113/JP287342
Muscle protein synthesis rates did not differ between follicular and luteal phases, showing that strength training works in every phase. What changes is recovery and injury risk.
Dot's translation You can train in every phase. But HOW you train should change. HIIT on Day 24 spikes cortisol in a body that's already managing enough. Strength training works all month. I adjust the intensity, not the ambition.

Mood & Emotional Regulation

6 studies
Serotonin, GABA, and dopamine fluctuate across your cycle. The emotional shifts are neurochemical, not personal. PeakHer validates what you're feeling and tells you why.
Pathophysiology of premenstrual syndrome and premenstrual dysphoric disorder
Rapkin AJ, Akopians AL
Menopause International, 2012 | DOI: 10.1258/mi.2012.012014
GABAergic and serotonergic neurotransmitter systems are the two most implicated in PMS/PMDD. Symptoms are triggered by post-ovulatory hormonal events, not abnormal hormone levels.
Effect of Estrogen-Serotonin Interactions on Mood and Cognition
Amin Z, Canli T, Epperson CN
Behavioral and Cognitive Neuroscience Reviews, 2005 | DOI: 10.1177/1534582305277152
Estrogen modulates serotonergic function through multiple mechanisms, explaining how estrogen fluctuations across the cycle affect mood.
Pathogenesis in menstrual cycle-linked CNS disorders
Backstrom T, Andreen L, Birzniece V, et al.
Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, 2003 | DOI: 10.1196/annals.1286.005
PMDD symptoms are linked to altered CNS sensitivity to allopregnanolone (a progesterone metabolite that modulates GABA-A receptors), not abnormal hormone levels.
Sex differences and stress across the lifespan
Bale TL, Epperson CN
Nature Neuroscience, 2015 | DOI: 10.1038/nn.4112
Sex differences in stress responses relate to both organizational and activational effects of gonadal hormones, creating sex-specific periods of vulnerability and resilience.
The Menstrual Cycle Influences Emotion but Has Limited Effect on Cognitive Function
Sundstrom-Poromaa I
Vitamins and Hormones, 2018 | DOI: 10.1016/bs.vh.2018.01.016
Emotional processing changes across the cycle are far more consistent than cognitive performance changes. The cycle is a better model for studying emotion than cognition.
Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors for premenstrual syndrome
Marjoribanks J, Brown J, O'Brien PMS, Wyatt K
Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, 2024 | DOI: 10.1002/14651858.CD001396
Cochrane meta-analysis confirming serotonergic dysfunction as central to PMS/PMDD, supporting the neurochemical basis of cycle-related mood changes.
Dot's translation You're not "being emotional." Your serotonin literally dipped. When I tell you to eat the sweet potatoes and skip the fight, it's because your neurochemistry shifted. I validate first, then redirect with an action.

Sleep & Recovery

5 studies
Progesterone raises your core body temperature in the luteal phase, disrupting the nighttime temperature drop you need for quality sleep. PeakHer accounts for this.
Circadian rhythms, sleep, and the menstrual cycle
Baker FC, Driver HS
Sleep Medicine, 2007 | DOI: 10.1016/j.sleep.2006.09.011
Subjective sleep quality is lowest around menses. Elevated progesterone in the luteal phase disrupts temperature regulation needed for sleep.
Circadian Variation of Sleep During the Follicular and Luteal Phases of the Menstrual Cycle
Shechter A, Varin F, Boivin DB
Sleep, 2010 | DOI: 10.1093/sleep/33.5.647
Core body temperature is elevated at night during the luteal phase with decreased temperature amplitude, while melatonin remains unaffected.
Objective Sleep Interruption and Reproductive Hormone Dynamics in the Menstrual Cycle
Sharkey KM, Crawford SL, Kim S, Joffe H
Sleep Medicine, 2014 | PMID: 24841109
Objective sleep interruption increases during the luteal phase and correlates with progesterone levels and basal body temperature changes.
Sleep and Women's Health
Nowakowski S, Meers J, Heimbach E
Sleep Medicine Research, 2013 | PMID: 25688326
Sleep complaints are 1.5-2x more common in women than men, with the menstrual cycle, pregnancy, and menopause as key hormonal contributors.
Sleep, Hormones, and Circadian Rhythms throughout the Menstrual Cycle
Shechter A, Boivin DB
International Journal of Endocrinology, 2010 | PMID: 20145718
Progesterone's thermogenic effect (raising core temp by ~0.4C in the luteal phase) disrupts the nighttime temperature drop needed for optimal sleep onset.
Dot's translation If you can't sleep during Sustain, it's not anxiety. It's progesterone. Your body temperature is literally too high for deep sleep. I'll adjust your evening routine and tell you why the melatonin isn't working.

Wearable Biometrics & Cycle Tracking

6 studies
Consumer wearables (Oura, Whoop, Garmin) can reliably detect cycle phases through HRV, skin temperature, and resting heart rate. This is why PeakHer integrates with them.
Wearable Sensors Reveal Menses-Driven Changes in Physiology and Enable Prediction of the Fertile Window
Goodale BM, Shilaih M, Falco L, et al.
Journal of Medical Internet Research, 2019 | DOI: 10.2196/13404
Wearable-measured temperature, pulse rate, respiratory rate, and HRV show significant luteal-phase shifts. Machine learning predicted the fertile window with 90% accuracy from wearable data alone.
Nocturnal finger skin temperature in menstrual cycle tracking: ambulatory pilot study using a wearable Oura ring
Grant AD, Newman M, Guan L, et al.
BMC Women's Health, 2019 | DOI: 10.1186/s12905-019-0844-9
The Oura ring detects the expected biphasic skin temperature pattern across the menstrual cycle, validating consumer wearables for cycle tracking.
Tracking Sleep, Temperature, Heart Rate, and Daily Symptoms Across the Menstrual Cycle with the Oura Ring
Alzueta E, Gombert-Labedens M, Javitz H, et al.
International Journal of Women's Health, 2022 | DOI: 10.2147/IJWH.S341917
Significant biphasic patterns: increased heart rate (p<0.03) and skin temperature (p<0.001) in the luteal phase. HRV showed corresponding decreases.
The Impact of Menstrual Cycle Phases on Cardiac Autonomic Nervous System Activity
Tada Y, Yoshizaki T, Tomata Y, et al.
Journal of Nutritional Science and Vitaminology, 2017 | DOI: 10.3177/jnsv.63.249
Menstrual-cycle-related variation in 24-hour HRV, with autonomic nervous system activity differing between follicular and luteal phases.
Modern fertility awareness methods: wrist wearables capture the changes in temperature associated with the menstrual cycle
Shilaih M, Goodale BM, Falco L, et al.
Bioscience Reports, 2018 | DOI: 10.1042/BSR20171279
Wrist-worn wearables accurately capture menstrual cycle temperature changes, establishing them as viable tools for cycle-phase identification.
Wearable-Derived Heart Rate Variability Across the Menstrual Cycle
Living Systematic Review (multiple authors)
Sports Medicine, 2025 | DOI: 10.1007/s40279-025-02388-y
HRV is higher at the beginning of the cycle and lower toward the end, with 3-9% differences between phases. Confirms wearable HRV as a reliable cycle-phase biomarker.

Gender Bias in Health Research

6 studies
The reason PeakHer needs to exist. Women have been systematically excluded from the research that built every health tool on the market.
Sex bias in neuroscience and biomedical research
Beery AK, Zucker I
Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews, 2011 | DOI: 10.1016/j.neubiorev.2010.07.002
Male bias in 8 of 10 biomedical disciplines. In neuroscience, single-sex studies of male animals outnumbered females 5.5 to 1.
A 10-year follow-up study of sex inclusion in the biological sciences
Woitowich NC, Beery AK, Woodruff TK
eLife, 2020 | DOI: 10.7554/eLife.56344
While sex inclusion improved after 2016 NIH policy, significant gaps remain. Many studies still fail to analyze results by sex even when both sexes are included.
NIH Revitalization Act of 1993
U.S. Congress (Public Law 103-43)
Signed June 10, 1993
First mandate requiring inclusion of women in NIH-funded research. Prior to this, a 1977 FDA policy recommended excluding women of childbearing potential from early drug trials.
Gender bias in research: how does it affect evidence based medicine?
Holdcroft A
Journal of the Royal Society of Medicine, 2007 | PMC: 1761670
Insights from male-only studies were routinely extrapolated to women despite meaningful biological differences in organs, genes, hormones, and disease presentation.
The Persistence of Sex Bias in High-Impact Clinical Research
Feldman S, Ammar W, Hardber L, et al.
PMC, 2025 | PMC: 11953574
Across high-impact clinical trials (2015-2019), significantly more males (56%) than females (44%) were included.
Inclusion, Analysis, and Reporting of Sex and Race/Ethnicity in Clinical Trials
Geller SE, Koch AR, Pellettieri B, Carnes M
Journal of Women's Health, 2011 | DOI: 10.1089/jwh.2010.2469
Fewer than 30% of participants in industry-sponsored early-phase trials were women, shaped by legal liability fears.
Dot's translation 85% of health research was done on men. Your workout plan, your nutrition guide, and your productivity system were designed for a body on a 24-hour hormonal cycle. Yours runs on 28 days. That's why I exist.

Perimenopause & Menopause

6 studies
Hormones don't disappear after menopause. They shift. The brain changes are measurable, real, and addressable. PeakHer is designed for every stage.
Menopause impacts human brain structure, connectivity, energy metabolism, and amyloid-beta deposition
Mosconi L, Berti V, Dyke J, et al.
Scientific Reports, 2021 | DOI: 10.1038/s41598-021-90084-y
Neuroimaging of 161 women (ages 40-65) showing substantial differences in brain structure, connectivity, and energy metabolism across menopause stages.
Perimenopause and Cognition
Greendale GA, Derby CA, Maki PM
Obstetrics and Gynecology Clinics of North America, 2011 | DOI: 10.1016/j.ogc.2011.05.007
Perimenopausal women were 1.4x more likely to report forgetfulness. Higher FSH and LH levels associated with worse verbal learning and memory.
Effects of the menopause transition and hormone use on cognitive performance in midlife women
Greendale GA, Huang MH, Wight RG, et al.
Neurology, 2009 | DOI: 10.1212/WNL.0b013e3181a71193
SWAN longitudinal study showing cognitive decrements during perimenopause not explained by mood, sleep, or vasomotor symptoms. Hormonal changes are direct contributors.
Menopausal Symptoms and Their Management
Santoro N, Epperson CN, Mathews SB
Endocrinology and Metabolism Clinics of North America, 2015 | DOI: 10.1016/j.ecl.2015.05.001
Comprehensive review of how hot flashes, mood disturbance, sleep disruption, and cognitive changes relate to hormonal mechanisms including serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine.
Menopausal hot flashes: Mechanisms, endocrinology, treatment
Freedman RR
Journal of Steroid Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, 2014 | DOI: 10.1016/j.jsbmb.2013.08.010
Estrogen withdrawal narrows the thermoneutral zone, making women's bodies hypersensitive to small temperature changes.
Hormone therapy and cognitive function
Maki PM, Sundermann E
Human Reproduction Update, 2009 | DOI: 10.1093/humupd/dmp022
Hormone therapy initiated early in perimenopause may benefit verbal memory, supporting a "critical window" hypothesis for intervention timing.
Dot's translation Your hormones didn't retire. They just changed jobs. The brain fog, the sleep disruption, the energy crashes ... they're real, they're measurable, and they're addressable. I still know what they're up to.

Cognition & Decision-Making

5 studies
The research on cognition is nuanced. Raw cognitive ability doesn't reliably shift across the cycle, but emotional processing, risk tolerance, and perception do. PeakHer focuses on how you show up, not what you're capable of.
Menstrual cycle effects on cognitive performance: A meta-analysis
Jang Y, Zhang L, Elfenbein HA
PLoS ONE, 2025 | DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0318576
Meta-analysis of 102 studies found no systematic evidence for significant cognitive performance shifts across the cycle. You're capable all month. How you FEEL changes.
Lack of Associations between Female Hormone Levels and Visuospatial Working Memory, Divided Attention and Cognitive Bias
Leeners B, Kruger THC, Geraedts K, et al.
Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience, 2017 | DOI: 10.3389/fnbeh.2017.00120
68 women tracked across two cycles showed no replicable effects of hormones on working memory, divided attention, or cognitive bias.
The Impact of Menstrual Cycle Phase on Economic Choice and Rationality
Lazzaro SC, Rutledge RB, Burghart DR, Glimcher PW
PLoS ONE, 2016 | DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0144080
Reduced loss aversion during the ovulatory phase compared to the luteal phase. Risk tolerance and economic decision-making shift across the cycle.
Evidence from neuroimaging for the role of the menstrual cycle in the interplay of emotion and cognition
Sacher J, Okon-Singer H, Villringer A
Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 2013 | DOI: 10.3389/fnhum.2013.00374
Neuroimaging shows cycle-related changes in brain regions involved in emotional processing (amygdala, hippocampus, prefrontal cortex), supporting emotional rather than cognitive variation.
Selective influence of menstrual cycle on perception of stimuli with reproductive significance
Krug R, Pietrowsky R, Fehm HL, Born J
Psychosomatic Medicine, 1994 | PMID: 7809340
Perception of specific stimulus categories shifted during the preovulatory phase, suggesting estrogen-driven perceptual changes near ovulation.
Dot's translation You're smart all month. That was never the question. What changes is how bold you feel, how risk-tolerant you are, and how your brain processes emotion. I don't tell you what you CAN do. I tell you what will feel easiest today.
