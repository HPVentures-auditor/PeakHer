## SECTION 1: LAUNCH PHASES

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Months 1-4 Before Public Launch)

**Primary Goal:** Build a waitlist of 1,000-5,000 women and validate core messaging.

**Secondary Goals:** Establish brand credibility, identify early advocates, refine the Rhythm Assessment quiz, and build an email list that converts.

#### 1.1 Landing Page and Lead Magnet

The centerpiece of pre-launch is a high-conversion landing page built around the **Rhythm Assessment** -- a 3-5 minute quiz that gives every woman immediate, personalized value before she ever downloads the app.

**Rhythm Assessment Quiz Design:**
- 12-15 questions covering energy patterns, sleep quality, performance consistency, stress response, and cycle awareness
- Questions are designed to create self-awareness ("Do you notice that some weeks you crush your to-do list and other weeks the same list feels impossible?")
- Results page delivers a personalized "Rhythm Profile" -- one of 4-6 archetypes (e.g., "The Surger," "The Steady Climber," "The Roller Coaster," "The Mystery Pattern")
- Each profile includes 2-3 immediately actionable insights and a clear call-to-action: "Want to decode your full pattern? Join the PeakHer waitlist."
- Quiz collects email, first name, and primary role (sales, athlete, entrepreneur, mom, executive, creative) for segmentation

**Landing Page Structure:**
- Headline: "What if you already had a performance pattern -- and you just didn't know it yet?"
- Sub-headline: "Take the 3-minute Rhythm Assessment and discover your unique performance profile."
- Social proof: early testimonials from beta testers, advisory board quotes, relevant statistics (81% of women report cycle-related productivity changes)
- Brief product preview: 2-3 app screen mockups showing the daily check-in, pattern dashboard, and weekly briefing
- Trust signals: privacy commitment, scientific approach, "not a period tracker" positioning
- Waitlist CTA with founding member pricing incentive

**Technical Requirements:**
- Built on a fast, lightweight stack (Webflow, Carrd, or custom Next.js page)
- Quiz powered by Typeform, Outgrow, or custom-built (custom preferred for data ownership)
- Email capture integrated with email marketing platform (ConvertKit or ActiveCampaign)
- UTM tracking on all inbound links for channel attribution
- Retargeting pixel installed (Meta, TikTok)

#### 1.2 Email Nurture Sequence

Every quiz completion triggers a 6-email nurture sequence delivered over 12 days. The sequence follows the Core Story framework -- educating first, selling second.

**Email 1 (Immediately after quiz): "Your Rhythm Profile"**
- Delivers the full Rhythm Profile results
- Frames the problem: "You're not broken. You're not lazy. You have a pattern -- you just haven't been able to see it."
- Introduces PeakHer as the tool that decodes her unique pattern
- Open rate target: 65%+

**Email 2 (Day 2): "The $115 Billion Productivity Myth"**
- Draws from Core Story Section 3 (Reason #1: The Performance Model Was Never Built for You)
- Shares the statistic: 81% of women report decreased productivity at some point during their cycle, yet 94.6% receive zero workplace support
- Reframes: the problem is not women's performance -- the problem is that performance tools ignore biology
- Links to a blog post or short video expanding on this data

**Email 3 (Day 4): "Why Your Best Week Isn't Random"**
- Draws from Core Story Section 4 (Reason #2: You're Running on One Speed)
- Introduces the concept of performance variability as a feature, not a bug
- Shares a brief case study or anonymized beta tester story: "Sarah tracked for 3 cycles and discovered her best client calls always happened in the same 5-day window. She moved her highest-stakes meetings there. Revenue went up 22%."
- Begins building desire for the product

**Email 4 (Day 7): "The Horoscope Problem"**
- Draws from Core Story Section 5 (Reason #3: Cycle Syncing Gives You a Horoscope, Not a Strategy)
- Explains the difference between prescriptive (one-size-fits-all) and descriptive/predictive (data-driven, personalized) approaches
- Positions PeakHer as the anti-horoscope: "We don't tell you what a textbook says about your phase. We show you what YOUR data reveals about YOUR patterns."
- This email differentiates PeakHer from every competitor

**Email 5 (Day 10): "The 6 Hats You Wear"**
- Draws from Core Story Section 7 (Reason #5: Nobody's Optimizing Across All the Hats)
- Addresses the multi-domain reality: a woman is not just a salesperson OR an athlete OR a mom -- she is all of these, and they interact
- Shows how PeakHer is the first tool designed for the whole woman, not a single slice of her life
- Includes a brief product walkthrough (GIF or 60-second video)

**Email 6 (Day 12): "Founding Members Get In First"**
- Direct offer: join the PeakHer beta at founding member pricing (50% off, locked for life)
- Creates urgency: limited to first 500 founding members
- Includes FAQ: privacy, how it works, what data is collected, how long until meaningful insights
- Clear CTA: "Claim Your Founding Member Spot"
- Conversion target: 8-12% of email list joins waitlist with founding member intent

### Phase 2: Closed Beta (1-2 Months Before Public Launch)

**Primary Goal:** 100-500 beta users providing daily feedback, validating core product assumptions, and generating case studies.

**Secondary Goals:** Stress-test the tech stack, refine the onboarding flow, calibrate the pattern detection engine, and build a community of early advocates.

#### 2.1 Beta User Selection

Invite beta users from the waitlist with intentional diversity across personas, demographics, and use cases.

**Selection Criteria:**
- Representation across all 6 personas (sales, athlete, entrepreneur, mom, executive, creative)
- Mix of cycle statuses: natural cycles, hormonal birth control, irregular cycles, perimenopause
- Geographic diversity (for timezone and cultural context testing)
- Mix of tech-savvy and non-tech-savvy users (the app must be intuitive for both)
- Willingness to commit to daily check-ins for the duration of beta (2 months minimum)

**Beta Cohort Structure:**
- Cohort 1 (Week 1): 50 users -- the "stress test" group. Heavy feedback expectations. Daily contact.
- Cohort 2 (Week 3): 100 additional users. More hands-off. Weekly surveys.
- Cohort 3 (Week 5): 200-350 additional users. Full beta population. In-app feedback only.
- Staggered rollout allows the team to fix critical issues before expanding.

#### 2.2 Feedback Mechanisms

**Weekly Feedback Surveys (5-7 minutes):**
- Delivered via email every Sunday evening
- Cover: ease of use, check-in friction, insight quality, feature requests, bugs, NPS
- Survey completion incentive: entered into monthly drawing for a year of free Premium

**In-App Feedback:**
- Persistent "feedback" button accessible from every screen
- After each insight is displayed: "Was this helpful?" (thumbs up / thumbs down / tell us more)
- After 7 days of consecutive check-ins: trigger a micro-survey ("How accurate are the insights so far? 1-5")
- After 30 days: trigger a longer reflection ("What has surprised you most about your patterns?")

**Private Community (Slack or Discord):**
- All beta users invited to a private Slack workspace (preferred over Discord for the professional audience)
- Channels: #general, #bugs, #feature-requests, #wins (share "aha moments"), #feedback, #introductions
- PeakHer team active daily in the community -- responding to every message within 4 hours during business hours
- Community becomes a source of testimonials, case studies, and word-of-mouth

**Beta User Interviews (10-15 in-depth):**
- Select 10-15 beta users for 30-45 minute video interviews at the 30-day and 60-day marks
- Focus areas: onboarding experience, daily habit formation, insight quality, emotional response to patterns, privacy concerns, willingness to pay, who they would recommend it to
- Record and transcribe all interviews (with permission) for product and marketing insights
- Compensate interview participants with lifetime founding member pricing

#### 2.3 Iteration Priorities During Beta

**Week 1-2: Onboarding and Check-in Flow**
- Is the onboarding completing at 80%+? If not, identify and fix drop-off points.
- Are users completing their first check-in within 24 hours of onboarding? If not, adjust notification timing and copy.
- Is the check-in taking under 30 seconds? If not, reduce friction.

**Week 3-4: Data Quality and Pattern Detection**
- Are users checking in daily? Track 7-day streak rates.
- Is the pattern detection engine surfacing meaningful patterns after 2 weeks of data?
- Are the early insights feeling accurate to users? Track "thumbs up" rates on insights.

**Week 5-6: Retention and Engagement**
- 30-day retention rate target: 50%+
- Are users who receive their first "aha moment" insight retaining at a higher rate? Quantify the difference.
- Is the weekly briefing being read? Track open and engagement rates.

**Week 7-8: Monetization Validation**
- Present premium feature previews to beta users
- Survey: "Would you pay $9.99/month for this?" Track responses by persona and engagement level.
- Identify which premium features drive the strongest purchase intent.

#### 2.4 Beta Success Criteria (Gates for Public Launch)

The following criteria must be met before proceeding to public launch:

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Onboarding completion rate | > 80% | Users must get through setup or they never activate |
| Day 1 check-in rate | > 70% | If users don't check in on day 1, they rarely come back |
| 7-day retention | > 50% | Health apps average 25%; PeakHer must beat the category |
| 30-day retention | > 35% | This indicates habit formation |
| Daily check-in completion (active users) | > 60% | The engine needs consistent data to generate insights |
| Insight "thumbs up" rate | > 65% | Insights must feel accurate and valuable |
| NPS | > 40 | Strong NPS indicates organic word-of-mouth potential |
| Crash rate | < 1% | App stability is table stakes |
| Critical bugs | 0 open | No launch-blocking issues |
| Willingness to pay (survey) | > 40% of active users | Validates the premium model |

### Phase 3: Public Launch

**Primary Goal:** 5,000-10,000 downloads in the first 30 days.

**Secondary Goals:** Achieve a 4.5+ App Store rating, generate press coverage, activate the referral engine, and convert 5-10% of downloads to premium.

#### 3.1 App Store Launch

**App Store Optimization (ASO):**
- App name: "PeakHer: Performance Intelligence"
- Subtitle: "Know Your Pattern. Own Your Performance."
- Keywords: cycle syncing, women's productivity, performance tracking, energy patterns, female optimization, cycle-based planning, women's wellness
- Category: Primary = Health & Fitness; Secondary = Productivity
- Screenshots: 6 screenshots showing (1) daily check-in, (2) pattern dashboard, (3) weekly briefing, (4) insight example, (5) multi-domain view, (6) privacy commitment
- App preview video: 30-second walkthrough showing the check-in, a pattern reveal, and the weekly briefing
- Description: lead with the value proposition, not features. "Discover your unique performance pattern. PeakHer learns from your daily data and reveals when you're built to push, create, connect, and rest."

**Launch on both platforms simultaneously:**
- iOS (App Store) -- primary platform, higher willingness to pay in health/wellness
- Android (Google Play) -- broader reach, important for diverse audience

**Pre-seeded reviews:**
- Ask 50+ beta users to leave honest App Store reviews on launch day
- Target: 50 reviews within the first 48 hours with an average of 4.5+ stars
- Do not incentivize specific star ratings -- ask for honest reviews and rely on beta satisfaction

#### 3.2 Launch Week Press Push

**Coordinated press outreach during launch week:**
- Embargo lifts at 9:00 AM ET on launch day
- Press kit includes: founder story, product screenshots, key statistics, competitive positioning, privacy commitment, download links
- Target 5-10 articles in the first week across women's health, business, fitness, and tech media
- Founder available for interviews and podcasts throughout launch week

**Launch day social media blitz:**
- Coordinated posts across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, and X (Twitter)
- Beta users and influencers post about the app (pre-coordinated, but in their own words)
- Founder posts a personal "why I built this" story on LinkedIn (target: viral reach in professional women's networks)

#### 3.3 Founding Member Pricing

**Offer structure:**
- Available to anyone who joined the waitlist before public launch OR downloads within the first 30 days
- 50% off Premium pricing, locked for life (as long as subscription remains active)
- Founding Member pricing: $4.99/month or $49/year (vs. standard $9.99/month or $99/year)
- Founding Members receive a permanent badge in the app and exclusive access to a Founding Members community channel

**Why this works:**
- Creates urgency: "This price disappears after launch month"
- Rewards early adopters and builds loyalty
- Generates word-of-mouth: founding members become evangelists because they feel ownership
- Locks in recurring revenue at a lower CAC than paid acquisition

**Founding Member cap:** No hard cap, but messaging implies scarcity ("Join the founding class of PeakHer"). In practice, the 30-day window is the natural cap.

#### 3.4 Referral Program

**Mechanics:**
- Every user gets a unique referral link and code
- Invite 3 friends who download and complete onboarding: earn 1 month of Premium free
- Referred friends receive their first month of Premium free
- Both sides benefit -- this is not a one-sided referral program

**Implementation:**
- In-app referral screen accessible from settings and profile
- Share via text, email, WhatsApp, Instagram DM, or copy link
- Referral dashboard showing invites sent, invites accepted, and rewards earned
- Push notification when a referral converts: "Your friend [Name] just joined PeakHer! You're 1 invite away from a free month of Premium."

**Referral Target:** 15-20% of new downloads come from referrals within the first 90 days.

#### 3.5 Launch Webinar / Virtual Event

**Format:** A live virtual event on launch day (or launch week), 60-90 minutes.

**Structure:**
- Opening: founder story and mission (10 minutes)
- Core Story presentation: condensed 20-minute version (Sections 1, 2, 5, 6, 7 from the full Core Story)
- Live product demo: walk through the app, show a real user's pattern dashboard (with permission), demonstrate a weekly briefing (15 minutes)
- Beta user testimonials: 2-3 beta users share their experience live (10 minutes)
- Q&A: live audience questions (15-20 minutes)
- Closing: founding member offer and download CTA (5 minutes)

**Promotion:**
- Email blast to full waitlist 7 days before, 3 days before, day of, and 1 hour before
- Social media countdown
- Partner organizations promote to their networks
- Registration target: 500-1,000 registrants
- Attendance target: 200-400 live attendees
- Replay sent to all registrants within 24 hours

#### 3.6 Email Blast to Full Waitlist

**Launch day email sequence:**
- Email 1 (Launch morning, 8:00 AM ET): "PeakHer Is Live. Download Now." Short, direct, download links for iOS and Android. Founding member pricing reminder.
- Email 2 (Launch day, 6:00 PM ET): "What People Are Saying" -- early reviews, social media reactions, download count milestone. Social proof.
- Email 3 (Day 3): "Your First Pattern Is Waiting" -- for waitlist members who have not yet downloaded. Emphasize that the sooner she starts tracking, the sooner she sees her patterns.
- Email 4 (Day 7): "Founding Member Pricing Ends In 23 Days" -- urgency email for non-converters.
- Email 5 (Day 25): "Last Chance: Founding Member Pricing Ends In 5 Days" -- final urgency push.

### Phase 4: Growth (Months 2-6 Post-Launch)

**Primary Goal:** 25,000+ total users, 2,000+ Premium subscribers, $15,000-$25,000 MRR.

**Secondary Goals:** Establish repeatable acquisition channels, build a content flywheel, launch team/enterprise offering, and prepare for Series A fundraising narrative.

#### 4.1 Paid Acquisition

**Channels (in priority order):**

1. **Instagram and Facebook Ads**
   - Creative: short-form video testimonials, before/after pattern reveals, quiz promotion
   - Targeting: women 25-45, interests in wellness, productivity, fitness, entrepreneurship, sales
   - Lookalike audiences built from waitlist, quiz completers, and paying subscribers
   - Budget: start at $2,000/month, scale to $5,000-$10,000/month based on CAC
   - Target CAC: under $15 for a free download, under $50 for a Premium conversion

2. **TikTok Ads**
   - Creative: native-style content that feels organic, not polished ads
   - Spark Ads: boost high-performing organic content from the PeakHer account and from influencer partners
   - Target demographic: women 22-38
   - Budget: start at $1,500/month, scale based on performance
   - Target CAC: under $10 for a free download (TikTok typically has lower CPMs)

3. **Podcast Ads**
   - Host-read ads on women's business, wellness, and fitness podcasts
   - 60-second mid-roll spots with unique promo codes for tracking
   - Target 5-10 podcasts per month in the $500-$2,000 per episode range
   - Budget: $5,000-$15,000/month
   - Podcast ads have high trust and tend to convert at higher rates than social

4. **Google Ads (Search)**
   - Target high-intent keywords: "cycle syncing app," "women's productivity app," "performance tracking for women," "cycle-based planning"
   - Budget: $1,000-$3,000/month
   - Lower priority than social and podcast due to smaller search volume in this category

#### 4.2 Content Flywheel

The content strategy matures from pre-launch awareness-building to a self-reinforcing flywheel.

**SEO Content (Blog):**
- Increase to 2-3 posts per week
- Target long-tail keywords with clear search intent
- Build topic clusters: "cycle syncing" cluster, "women's performance" cluster, "working mom productivity" cluster, "female athlete training" cluster
- Each post includes a CTA to download the app or take the Rhythm Assessment quiz
- Goal: 10,000+ organic monthly visitors by month 6

**YouTube Channel:**
- Launch a YouTube channel with weekly content
- Content types: "What Your Pattern Says About You" (pattern profiles), founder Q&As, user story features, science explainers, "PeakHer in Action" product walkthroughs
- YouTube is the long-term SEO play -- videos surface in Google search results
- Budget: $1,000-$2,000/month for editing and production

**Podcast (Owned):**
- Consider launching a PeakHer-branded podcast: "The Pattern" or "Many Hats"
- Format: 20-30 minute episodes, weekly
- Content: interviews with women across all personas about their performance patterns, conversations with researchers, practical tips
- Podcast builds brand authority and creates a content asset that compounds over time
- Decision gate: launch only if the team has bandwidth; a mediocre podcast is worse than no podcast

#### 4.3 Partnership Expansion

**Gyms and Fitness Studios:**
- Partner with boutique fitness studios (CrossFit, barre, cycling, yoga) to offer PeakHer as a member benefit
- Studio posts a QR code in the locker room or includes PeakHer in the new-member welcome packet
- PeakHer provides free Premium for all active studio members (subsidized by the studio at a bulk rate)
- Target: 10-20 studio partnerships in the first 6 months

**Coaching Programs:**
- Partner with business coaches, executive coaches, and life coaches who serve women
- Coaches recommend PeakHer to their clients as a tracking and self-awareness tool
- PeakHer provides coach-specific features (client pattern reports, shared dashboards) in Phase 2 product roadmap
- Revenue share: coaches earn 20% of referred Premium subscriptions for 12 months

**Corporate Wellness Programs:**
- Approach corporate wellness buyers at companies with strong women's ERGs
- Offer pilot programs: 50-100 employees at a company trial PeakHer for 3 months, with aggregate (anonymized) reporting on engagement and satisfaction
- If the pilot succeeds, convert to a paid team/enterprise contract
- Target: 3-5 corporate pilots by month 6

#### 4.4 Team/Enterprise Sales

**Target Buyers:**
- Sales managers and VPs of Sales at companies with significant female sales teams
- Directors of corporate wellness programs
- Chief People Officers and HR leaders at companies with diversity and inclusion initiatives
- Coaching organizations and leadership development firms

**Sales Motion:** Bottom-up (preferred for months 2-6).
- Individual women discover PeakHer, use it, love it, and advocate for a team deployment
- When 5+ women at the same company are using PeakHer individually, the sales team reaches out to the company's HR/wellness buyer with a team proposal
- Bottom-up adoption provides built-in proof of value

**Sales Motion:** Top-down (introduced in month 4-6).
- Direct outreach to corporate wellness and HR buyers
- Offer a free pilot for the women's ERG or sales team
- Provide anonymized aggregate data on team engagement (not individual health data -- this is critical for privacy)

#### 4.5 Community Building

**The "PeakHer Collective":**
- Transition the beta Slack community into a broader user community
- Open to all Premium members
- Channels organized by persona: #sales, #athlete, #entrepreneur, #mom, #executive, #creative
- Weekly community events: "Pattern Share Monday" (users share their latest insight), "Ask the Team Wednesday" (Q&A with PeakHer product team)
- Community moderators recruited from power users (compensated with free Premium)

**User-Generated Content:**
- Encourage users to share their pattern dashboards and insights on social media (with a built-in "share to Instagram Stories" feature in the app)
- Create a branded hashtag: #MyRhythm or #FindYourPeakHer
- Feature user stories on the PeakHer blog and social accounts (with permission)
- UGC is the highest-trust, lowest-cost content source

She is competitive, driven, and measured by numbers. She notices that some weeks she is unstoppable on calls and other weeks she cannot seem to close. **Where she hangs out:**

- Offline: sales kickoff events, industry conferences (Dreamforce, SaaStr, INBOUND), local networking events, gym or running club (stress relief from a demanding role)

**Best channels to reach her:**
1. LinkedIn (organic thought leadership + paid ads targeting "sales" job titles)

**Partnerships to pursue:**

She tracks her workouts, cares about performance data, and has likely heard of cycle-syncing for training. ## SECTION 4: PRICING STRATEGY

### 4.1 Free Tier

**What is included:**
- Daily check-in (energy, confidence, stress, sleep quality, cycle day)
- Basic cycle tracking and phase estimation
- 7-day energy trend view
- Monthly summary (basic)
- Integration with one wearable or calendar

**Why the free tier exists:**
1. **Drive adoption:** Removing the paywall for basic tracking eliminates the biggest barrier to download. The app needs data volume to generate insights -- free users provide data even if they never pay.
2. **Build the habit:** The daily check-in habit is the foundation of everything. Free users who check in for 14+ consecutive days are primed for conversion because they have begun to see patterns.
3. **Collect data for the intelligence engine:** Every check-in, regardless of the user's subscription tier, feeds the aggregate pattern detection model. Free users make the product better for everyone.
4. **Word-of-mouth:** Free users are still evangelists. A woman who checks in daily and sees basic trends will tell her friends.

**Conversion triggers (what makes free users upgrade):**
- After 14 days of check-ins: "You've built enough data for PeakHer to detect your first pattern. Upgrade to Premium to see it." (This is the most powerful trigger -- the pattern is already computed; the user just needs to unlock it.)
- After a "power week" or "low week": "Your data shows this was one of your highest-energy weeks in the last 30 days. Want to know when the next one is coming? Upgrade to see your prediction."
- After 30 days: "Here's your monthly summary. Premium members see cross-domain correlations, weekly briefings, and predictive insights. Try Premium free for 7 days."
- After adding a second life domain (e.g., fitness + work): "You're tracking across two domains. Premium unlocks cross-domain pattern analysis -- see how your best workout weeks correlate with your best work weeks."
- After a friend refers them: the referred user receives 1 month of Premium free, creating a low-risk trial.

### 4.2 Premium ($9.99/month)

**What unlocks at Premium:**
- Full pattern dashboard: all detected patterns across all life domains
- Weekly briefing: AI-generated preview of the coming week based on predicted energy, confidence, and performance windows
- Cross-domain correlations: "Your best sales weeks tend to be the same weeks you run your fastest 5Ks"

**Pricing psychology -- why $9.99/month:**
- $9.99 sits at the psychological threshold of "under $10" -- it feels like a small, justifiable expense

- It is below the "need to ask my partner" threshold for most target users -- she can decide on her own
- The goal is volume at this stage, not margin. Converting 10% of free users at $9.99/month builds a meaningful MRR base quickly.

**Annual discount: $99/year ($8.25/month)**
- 17% savings vs. monthly pricing
- Annual pricing achieves two goals: (1) reduces churn (annual subscribers churn at roughly 1/3 the rate of monthly), and (2) improves cash flow and LTV
- Messaging: "Save $20/year with an annual plan" (framing the savings in absolute dollars, not percentages, feels more tangible)

**Premium pricing justification for the user:**
- Cost per day: $0.33 (less than a third of a coffee)
- If PeakHer helps a saleswoman close one additional deal per quarter by scheduling high-stakes calls during her peak performance windows, the ROI is 100x+
- If PeakHer helps a runner avoid one injury per year by identifying over-training patterns, it pays for itself in avoided medical costs and lost training time

## APPENDIX: KEY MESSAGING FRAMEWORK

### The One-Liner
"PeakHer is a personal performance intelligence platform that helps women discover their unique patterns across every role they play."

### The Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)
"Every woman knows that some weeks she is on fire -- at work, at the gym, with her family -- and other weeks she struggles to keep up. But she has no idea why. PeakHer is the first app that helps women discover their unique performance patterns by tracking daily energy, outcomes, and biometrics across every area of life. Unlike generic cycle-syncing apps that give the same advice to every woman, PeakHer learns from YOUR data and reveals YOUR patterns. It's not a period tracker. It's a performance intelligence platform."

### The Stadium Pitch (Core Story opener)
"I'm here to share the 5 reasons high-performing women burn out -- and what the top performers do differently to sustain peak performance across every role they play. This isn't about working less. It's about working WITH your biology instead of against it."

### Positioning Against Competitors

**vs. Period Trackers (Flo, Clue):**
"Period trackers tell you where you are in your cycle. PeakHer tells you what your cycle means for your performance -- and what to do about it."

**vs. Generic Cycle-Syncing Apps (Phase, Closer):**
"Most cycle-syncing apps give you the same advice as every other woman in your phase. PeakHer learns from YOUR data and reveals YOUR individual patterns. It's the difference between a horoscope and a personal performance report."

**vs. Fitness Apps (Wild.AI, Fitr Woman):**
"Fitness apps optimize one domain. You don't live in one domain. PeakHer optimizes across work, fitness, family, creativity, and leadership -- and shows you how they connect."

**vs. Wearables (Whoop, Oura):**
"Whoop and Oura collect incredible data. PeakHer makes that data meaningful. We integrate with your wearable and add the intelligence layer -- correlating your biometrics with your actual performance outcomes across every area of life."

### Key Phrases and Language Rules

**Always say:**
- "Your pattern" (personalized)
- "Performance intelligence" (not wellness)
- "Strategic advantage" (not limitation)
- "Data-driven" (not prescriptive)
- "Every woman's pattern is unique" (never generalize)
- "Works for all women" (inclusive of hormonal BC, irregular cycles, perimenopause)

**Never say:**
- "Your hormones are making you..." (blaming biology)
- "You should rest during your period" (prescriptive)
- "Women are more creative during ovulation" (overgeneralized)
- "Cycle syncing" as a standalone descriptor (we are more than this)
- "Track your period" (we are not a period tracker)
- "Optimize your hormones" (medical claim territory)
