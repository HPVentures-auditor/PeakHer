## SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING (EXPANDED)

For every objection: what they say, why they are really saying it, how to respond, how to reframe, and the data point that supports you.

---

### OBJECTION 1: "Isn't this just another period tracker?"

**Why they are saying it:** They are mentally filing PeakHer in the same category as Flo and Clue. They already have a period tracker or do not want one. They are telling you: I do not see how this is different from what I already know exists.

**The response:** "I understand why it sounds that way, and I am glad you asked. Period trackers tell you when your period is coming. Full stop. PeakHer tells you when your best performance is coming -- your highest energy, your peak confidence, your best days for closing deals or hitting PRs or being patient with your kids. Your period is one data point. PeakHer connects that data point to your actual outcomes across every role you play. It is the difference between a thermometer and a weather forecast."

**The reframe:** "A period tracker watches one signal. PeakHer connects that signal to everything else in your life and shows you the pattern. Nobody downloads a period tracker and discovers that their close rate is 3x higher on certain days. That is what PeakHer does."

**Supporting data:** Zero existing period trackers correlate cycle data with real-world performance outcomes (market research, 2026). Flo has 420M+ downloads but offers zero performance intelligence. The gap is not tracking -- it is meaning.

---

### OBJECTION 2: "I'm on birth control -- does this work for me?"

**Why they are saying it:** They think this is a cycle-syncing tool and that hormonal birth control disqualifies them. They have been excluded before by apps or frameworks that assume a natural 28-day cycle.

**The response:** "Yes, and this is one of the things that makes PeakHer fundamentally different. Your menstrual cycle is one signal, but it is not the only signal. PeakHer tracks your energy, your confidence, your sleep, your stress, your calendar, and your real-world outcomes. Those patterns exist whether or not you have a natural hormonal cycle. Women on birth control still have rhythms -- they are just driven by different inputs. PeakHer finds them regardless."

**The reframe:** "Most cycle-syncing tools only work for women with textbook 28-day cycles. That excludes roughly 30% of reproductive-age women. We built PeakHer to work for all women -- because every woman has a rhythm, whether or not she has a natural period."

**Supporting data:** Approximately 14% of US women aged 15-49 use hormonal birth control (CDC). PeakHer's pattern recognition engine is designed to discover energy and performance rhythms from behavioral data alone, independent of cycle input.

---

### OBJECTION 3: "I don't want to be told I can't do something because of my period."

**Why they are saying it:** This is the deepest objection. She is worried this tool will reinforce the narrative that women are limited by their biology. She has spent her career fighting that perception and does not want an app telling her to sit down when she has a board meeting to run.

**The response:** "Neither do we. That is literally our first design principle: empowerment, not limitation. PeakHer never tells you what you cannot do. It tells you what you are uniquely primed to do. There is no screen in the app that says 'do not schedule this meeting' or 'avoid this task.' It says: 'based on your data, your confidence and close rate are historically highest on days like today.' You decide what to do with that information. This is about giving you more intelligence, not less freedom."

**The reframe:** "Think of it like a weather forecast. Nobody hears 'it will rain Thursday' and thinks the weather is telling them they cannot go outside. They bring an umbrella. That is what PeakHer does for your performance -- it gives you the forecast so you can make better decisions. Not fewer decisions. Better ones."

**Supporting data:** The Jang et al. (2025) meta-analysis of 102 studies found no robust evidence that women's cognitive ability changes across the cycle. What changes is energy, mood, and communication style -- and those are strategic variables, not limitations.

### OBJECTION 4: "The science on cycle syncing seems mixed."

That is exactly why we do not give generic phase-based advice. That model is not scientifically supported. What IS supported is that women experience real, consistent variation in energy, mood, stress tolerance, and communication style across their cycle. And the most important finding across virtually every study is that individual variation is enormous. PeakHer does not use group averages. It discovers YOUR patterns from YOUR data. That is not a scientific claim about all women. It is your personal evidence about you."

**The reframe:** "The science actually supports our approach better than any other product in this space. The research says: generic phase advice does not hold up. Individual patterns are what matter. That is literally what PeakHer is built to find."

Emotional processing and subjective energy variation are better supported (Sundstrom-Poromaa, 2018). Individual variation overwhelms group averages in virtually every study.

This is the scientific justification for our personalized, data-driven approach rather than generic phase-based advice.
