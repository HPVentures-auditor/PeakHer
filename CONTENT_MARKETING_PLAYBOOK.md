### Blog Post

**Title:** "The Hidden Performance Tax: How Your Cycle Costs You 23 Days a Year"

**Word count target:** 1,800-2,200 words

**Outline:**

1. **Opening hook:** You lost 23 days of peak performance last year. Not to vacation. Not to illness. To a biological tax nobody told you about.
2. **The data wall:** Present the Schoep et al. (2019) finding -- 81% of women report decreased productivity due to their cycle, translating to 23.2 days per year. Cite the sample size (32,748 women) for credibility.
3. **The support gap:** 94.6% of women receive zero workplace support for cycle-related issues. 75.6% want it but have never been offered any (Ponzo et al., 2022).
4. **The "always on" trap:** Explain the 24-hour clock vs. 28-day clock distinction. Every productivity system, sales methodology, and training plan was designed for a daily hormonal reset. Women operate on a monthly rhythm that nobody accounts for.
5. **The cost to employers and women themselves:** Connect to the McKinsey data -- 42% of women report burnout vs. 35% of men. Burnout is the #1 reason women leave their employers.
6. **The irony:** Women-founded startups generate 2.5x more revenue per dollar invested (BCG, 2018). The problem is not capability. It is sustainability.
7. **The reframe:** These 23 days are not lost. They are misallocated. The women who understand their rhythm do not lose those days -- they use them differently.
8. **CTA:** Introduce the concept of Performance Modes. Link to the Performance Modes Guide (lead magnet). Invite readers to take the Rhythm Assessment Quiz.

**Secondary keywords:** "women burnout statistics," "cycle and work performance," "hidden cost of periods at work"

   - Subjective experience is real: Women consistently report variation in energy, mood, stress tolerance, and motivation across their cycle. Too consistent and widespread to dismiss.
   - Emotional and motivational shifts have better support than cognitive ones: Changes in emotional processing, risk tolerance, and motivation have more empirical backing than changes in raw cognitive ability (Sundstrom-Poromaa, 2018; Lazzaro et al., 2016; Cook and Crewther, 2019).

4. **The key finding that changes everything:** Individual variation overwhelms group averages. Some women experience dramatic shifts; others notice almost nothing.
5. **Why this supports PeakHer's approach:** Prescriptive apps assume Phase X means Outcome Y for all women. The science does not support this. What the science DOES support is personalized tracking -- letting each woman discover her own patterns from her own data. This is the only scientifically honest approach.
6. **The ethics:** PeakHer never claims biology dictates capability. Every insight is framed as information, not limitation.
