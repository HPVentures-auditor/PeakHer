# PeakHer Research Notes

## Human History Timeline
- Anatomically modern humans (Homo sapiens): ~300,000 years (oldest fossils: Jebel Irhoud, Morocco)
- Genus Homo (Homo erectus, Homo habilis, etc.): ~2 to 2.5 million years

## Global Progress Stats (Mixed Picture)

### Getting Better
- **Extreme poverty:** Down from ~36% (1990) to under 10% (pre-COVID)
- **Child mortality:** ~19M deaths/year (1960) down to ~5M (2020s)
- **Literacy:** Global adult literacy ~56% (1950) to ~87% today
- **Life expectancy:** Global average ~47 years (1950) to ~73 years
- **Clean water access:** 90%+ now have basic access, up from ~76% (1990)
- **Democracy:** More people under democratic governance than ever (though plateauing)
- **Violence:** Long-term per-capita decline in deaths from war and violence (Pinker)

### Getting Worse (or Stalled)
- **Climate change:** CO2, temperatures, extreme weather all trending up
- **Biodiversity loss:** Extinction rates 100-1,000x natural baseline
- **Mental health:** Depression, anxiety, loneliness rising, especially in young people
- **Inequality:** Within-country wealth gaps widened since 1980s
- **Democratic backsliding:** 18+ consecutive years of global decline (Freedom House)
- **Debt:** Global debt-to-GDP at historic highs
- **Trust:** Declining trust in institutions (media, government, science)

**Takeaway (Hans Rosling / Factfulness):** The world is both better than most people think and still full of serious problems. Material/health basics improved enormously. Ecological, psychological, and political foundations under real strain.

## Men's Testosterone Decline with Age

**Pattern:** Peaks in late teens to early 20s, then declines ~1-2% per year after age 30.

| Age   | Typical Range (ng/dL) |
|-------|-----------------------|
| 20s   | 600-900               |
| 30s   | 500-800               |
| 40s   | 450-700               |
| 50s   | 400-600               |
| 60s   | 350-550               |
| 70s+  | 250-450               |

- **Low T threshold:** Generally below ~300 ng/dL
- **Free testosterone** drops even faster (SHBG binding protein increases with age)
- **Huge individual variation.** Sleep, exercise, body fat, stress, and diet accelerate or slow decline
- **Obesity** is a major accelerator. Excess body fat converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase
- **Generational decline:** Men today may have lower baselines than same-age men 30-40 years ago (causes debated: endocrine disruptors, obesity rates, lifestyle)
- Decline is gradual enough that many men don't notice until it's dropped substantially
